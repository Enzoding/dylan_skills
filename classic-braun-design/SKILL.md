# Classic Braun Design

## Purpose

Translate classic Braun physical product language into digital interfaces. This is an art-direction skill for strong visual output, not a general taste audit. It should make websites, apps, and pages feel like carefully designed Braun products while preserving modern usability.

This skill inherits the useful, clear, honest, restrained, durable spirit of `less-but-better-design`, but it is independently usable and more visually specific.

## Core Rule

When this skill is explicitly invoked, let classic Braun product language lead the visual direction. Do not merely make the interface "minimal." Make it product-like: precise, modular, tactile, labeled, measured, and functional.

Use the full design vocabulary selectively. Choose 2-4 dominant Braun traits for the current interface, then keep the rest quiet. Do not apply every trait at once.

## Workflow

1. Identify the interface's primary job and required states before styling it.
2. Choose a product-like framing for the UI: panel, module system, dial face, control surface, product catalog, instrument readout, or compact device face.
3. Select 2-4 dominant traits from the product language: modular panel grid, circular control, perforation grid, segmented controls, small technical labels, functional accent color, measured scale, matte casing, clear acrylic layer, or calculator-like keys.
4. Translate the traits into digital patterns without harming usability: navigation, cards, forms, buttons, dashboards, mobile controls, data displays, and responsive layouts must still work naturally.
5. Apply `less-but-better-design` principles in the background: remove noise, keep hierarchy clear, stay honest, preserve necessary complexity, and resolve details.
6. Avoid nostalgia, generic retro styling, and modern tech-gloss. The result should feel classic, clean, engineered, and current enough to use.

## Output Behavior

Do not explain historical product models unless the user asks. Use accessible shape language: radio face, calculator keys, clock dial, control panel, device label, module, grille, indicator light.

Do not include images or require image assets. Generate the style through layout, CSS, typography, color, components, states, and interaction details.

Do not produce a checklist or score. Let the artifact carry the style. If explanation is useful, briefly name the selected traits and why they fit the interface.
